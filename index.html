    NYC Schools Surveys and What We Can Learn from Them

    Many studies on achievment gap don't necessarily lead to actionable results. In this project, I analyzed the aggregated data from NYC Schools Surveys in order to predict student performance scores in State Assessment Tests. I tested whether the opinions of students give an insight into the school evnironment in which the students are learning. I found that how safe students feel in and around their schools is a strong predictor of how well they perform. I generated a map in which school adiministrators, district adiministrators, parents, and teachers can see these results. My hope is that this can be an aiding tool for the schools and districts to address safety issues.

    See Project Details Here

    Risk Assessment Based on Multiple Disasters in California

    In 2020, the COVID-19 pandemic has rampaged throughout the world and cause havoc on people's lives, the healthcare system, and the economy. We don't know how the combination of other natural disasters with COVID-19 would affect a region. In this team project, we explored the effects of COVID-19, wildfires, and earthquakes in the state of California. We generated a map in which a person can see the combined risk index (that we generated) for each county. This map is a tool that can be used to make a county-level preparedness plan.

    See Project Details Here

    The Onion vs News

    In this project, I analyzed the text from two subreddit feeds: r/TheOnion and r/news. It is often challenging to pick up satire and sarcasm in text. (The Onion is a satirical news media.) By using machine learning, I investigated whether it can differenciate between satire and real news. To my surprise, the models predicted the origin of text with upwards of almost 90% accuracy. The texts that were important contributors to the predictions were not always clear into why they heavily contributed to the model (e.g. Apple). This was a fun project to work on. However, it gives a great insight into the power of NLP models that I used for this project.
